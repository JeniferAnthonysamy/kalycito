History of Industrial Revolutions…

Industry Revolution 1.0

The first Industrial Revolution started with invention of mechanical production powered by water and steam

Industry Revolution 2.0

The invention of electricity in 1900s led to mass production, with machines powered by electricity and combustion engines powering assembly lines for manufacturing

Industry Revolution 3.0

The 1970s were characterized by the advent of electronics, IT, and industrial robotics for advanced automation of production processes with operations shifting from manual paper based to sophisticated ERP systems

Industry Revolution 4.0

Starting post 2010, companies are moving towards a digital supply chain where machines communicate with each other not just within the company but also with extended supply chains

"The business goal of the digital supply chain is to deliver the right product into the customer’s hands as quickly as possible — but also to do so responsively and reliably while increasing efficiency and cutting costs through automation."

Era of convergence

Since no one company can drive such a massive transformation, the Industrial Internet Consortium (IIC) was born. The IIC is a group of companies aiming for an open, unified, standards-based and interoperable IIoT solution for deterministic communication between industrial devices and to the cloud.
